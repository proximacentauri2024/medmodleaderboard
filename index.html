MedMod

Clinical practice involves the analysis of information from multiple sources to make informed decisions about the patient's state. Recently, multimodal machine learning has provided new opportunities to developing models that integrate multiple modalities, mimicking clinical practice. However, multimodal benchmarks involving routine modalities, specifically Electronic Health Records (EHR) and Chest X-ray images (CXR), are scarce.

To help advance multimodal learning in tackling real-world prediction tasks, we present MedMod with the following contributions:

  • We propose a diverse and comprehensive multimodal clinical benchmark comprising six clinical prediction tasks using two publicly available datasets MIMIC-IV and MIMIC-CXR: prediction of clinical conditions, in-hospital mortality, decompensation, length of stay, readmission, and radiological findings.
  • We perform extensive evaluations of six supervised learning models, encompassing basic fusion paradigms (early, joint, late) and other sophisticated multimodal frameworks.
  • We also evaluate three state-of-the-art self-supervised learning methods and present a unified evaluation scheme and protocol for each task.
